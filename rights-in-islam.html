Rights in Islam

A comprehensive system of rights and responsibilities for all members of society

Islam has prescribed that its followers give people their due rights. Parents, spouses, children, neighbors - all are given specific rights according to their specific roles in this world. This establishes mutual bonds and solidarity between individuals of a Muslim society.

"And worship Allah [alone], and do not ascribe partners to Him. And do good unto your parents and near of kin and unto orphans, and the needy, and the neighbor from among your own people, and the neighbor who is a stranger, and the friend by your side, and the wayfarer, and those whom your right hands possess. Verily, Allah does not love those who are full of self-conceit and boastful."

The Rights of Public Paths

Even public spaces have rights that must be respected. The Messenger of Allah, Muhammad (peace be upon him) said:

"Be careful and stay away from sitting on the paths." They said, "O Messenger of Allah, we have no other place to gather and talk." So he replied, "If you must do so, then give the path its right." They said, "And what is the right of the path?" He replied, "Lowering one's gaze (not looking at members of the opposite sex), not bringing harm to others, replying to salutations, and enjoining good and forbidding evil."

‚Äî Sahih Bukhari

Rights of Public Spaces:

  • Lowering one's gaze - Maintaining modesty and respect
  • Not bringing harm to others - Ensuring safety and cleanliness
  • Replying to salutations - Promoting social harmony
  • Enjoining good and forbidding evil - Maintaining moral standards

üêæ Animal Rights and Compassion

Islam extends its system of rights even to animals, showing the comprehensive nature of Islamic mercy and justice.

üèÜ Reward for Kindness to Animals

"Once a man was tending to some business, and became very thirsty. He came across a well, so he descended into it and drank from it. [When he came out], he saw a dog panting and rolling his mouth in dirt (in search of water) due to thirst. The man said, 'This dog is as thirsty as I was.' So he descended [once again] filled his boot with water and gave drink to the dog. [Due to that] Allah rewarded him by forgiving his sins."

They said, "O Messenger of Allah, will we be rewarded even due to our well-treatment of animals?" He replied, "Yes indeed. For [the well-treatment of] every fresh liver (living thing) you will receive reward."

‚Äî Sahih Bukhari

"A woman was punished due to a cat. She imprisoned it until it died, so she entered the Hellfire because of this. She did not feed it nor give it drink when she kept it, nor did she let it free to eat insects (and other things) of the earth."

Principles of Animal Rights in Islam:

  • Providing food and water to animals in need
  • Not causing unnecessary harm or suffering
  • Proper care for animals under human custody
  • Allowing animals freedom when possible
  • Showing compassion to all living creatures
