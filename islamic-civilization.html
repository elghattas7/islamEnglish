Islamic Civilization

The golden age that illuminated the world with knowledge and scientific advancement

Muslims had advanced in all technical, scientific and intellectual fields during the golden age of Islamic civilization. Their contributions laid the foundation for modern science and continue to benefit humanity today.

üî¢ Mathematics and Astronomy

Al-Khawarizmi (780-850 CE)

Mathematics & Algebra

A great scholar in mathematics, algebra, logarithms and geometry. He was perhaps one of the greatest mathematicians who ever lived and was the founder of Algebra.

Key Contributions:
  • Founded several branches of mathematics
  • Developed algebraic concepts
  • Advanced geometric principles

Al-Biruni (973-1050 CE)

Astronomy & Mathematics

A great scholar in many fields, writing on topics from astronomy to mathematics, mathematical geography to mechanics, pharmacology and history.

Revolutionary Discovery:
  • Discussed earth's rotation 600 years before Galileo
  • Advanced mathematical geography
  • Contributed to mechanics and pharmacology

"He was the greatest intellectual known to man." - German Orientalist E. Sachau

Al-Hasan Ibn Al-Haitham (Alhazen) (965-1040 CE)

Optics & Scientific Method

A great mathematician and pioneer in optics, engineering and astronomy. His work laid the foundation for the modern scientific method.

Groundbreaking Work:
  • First to explain apparent size changes of celestial bodies
  • Wrote "Kitab Al-Manadhir" (Book of Optics)
  • Pioneered experimental scientific method

‚öïÔ∏è Medicine and Healthcare

Muslim scholars left behind a wealth of knowledge in medicine and pharmacy, which were used to advance modern day medicine.

Ibn Rushd (Averroes) (1126-1198 CE)

An Andalusian (Spanish) philosopher and physician, master of philosophy and Islamic law, mathematics and medicine.

Ibn An-Nafees (1213-1288 CE)

Famous for being the first to describe the pulmonary circulation of the blood. He discovered blood circulation before Harvey and Michael Servetus by hundreds of years.

Ammaar Ibn Ali Al-Mosili

Highly skilled in ophthalmology. He invented specialized instruments used in operations, such as the "injection syringe" - a hollow needle.

Al-Mansoori & Abu Bakr Ar-Razi

Renowned, versatile physicians who made fundamental and enduring contributions to medicine and philosophy.

Muwaffaq Al-Baghdadi & Abul-Qasim Az-Zahrawi

Renowned in dental practices. They wrote illustrated books about surgical tools and procedures.

Shareef Al-Idrisi (1100-1165 CE)

Cartography & Geography

A cartographer, geographer and traveler renowned for his excellent maps of the world. He also invented navigational instruments that aided exploration and trade.
