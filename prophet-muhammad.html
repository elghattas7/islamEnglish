Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

The Final Messenger of Islam - A man of truth, trustworthiness, and mercy

He is Abul-Qasim, Muhammad, the son of Abdullah, the son of Abdul-Muttalib, the son of Hashim. He belonged to the Arab tribe of Quraish whose ancestry traces back to Adnan, one of the children of Ishmael, the Prophet of Allah.

üåü Birth and Early Life

Birth and Lineage

  • Born in the year 571 C.E. in the honored city of Makkah
  • Makkah was the religious center of the Arabian Peninsula
  • It housed the Honored Ka'bah, built by Abraham and his son Ishmael
  • His ancestry traces back to Ishmael, son of Abraham

Character Before Prophethood

Even before he received revelation, he was known amongst his people for his exceptional character:

  • 'As-Sadiq' (The Truthful) - He never lied
  • 'Al-Amin' (The Trustworthy) - People entrusted him with their possessions
  • He never acted treacherously
  • He always desired good for others

üìñ The First Revelation

He received his first revelation at the age of forty. When he returned home, he informed his wife, Khadijah (may Allah be pleased with her), apprehensively:

"Indeed I fear for myself."

Khadijah replied with words that show the Prophet's noble character:

"No, by Allah! Allah will never humiliate you. Indeed you maintain ties of kinship, you bear the problems of others, you give wealth to those who do not have it, you honor and feed your guests, and you give support in times of true calamity."

13

Years in Makkah

He remained in Makkah for thirteen years, calling people to believe in the Oneness of Allah, facing persecution but never giving up his mission.

Migration to Medina

He migrated to the city of Medina and called its inhabitants to Islam, which they accepted. There, Allah revealed to him the remaining legislations of the religion.

Return to Makkah

He returned and conquered Makkah eight years after his migration, forgiving his former enemies and establishing justice.

63

Completion of Mission

He died at the age of sixty-three after Allah revealed to him the entire Quran. All legislations of the religion were perfected and completed.

The Final Messenger

Muslims believe that Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the final messenger sent by Allah to all of humanity. His message completes and confirms the messages brought by previous prophets including Abraham, Moses, and Jesus (peace be upon them all).
