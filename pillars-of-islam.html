The Five Pillars of Islam

The fundamental practices that form the foundation of Muslim faith and worship

Physical and verbal acts of worship are called the Pillars of Islam. There are five Pillars of Islam that form the foundation of Muslim life and practice.

1

The Testimony of Faith (Shahada)

This is the testimony that 'none has the right to be worshipped but Allah, and that Muhammad is His slave and Messenger'. This testimony is the key with which one becomes a Muslim.

لا إله إلا الله محمد رسول الله

Lā ilāha illā Allah, Muḥammad rasūl Allah

"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the messenger of Allah"

The meaning of the first part: "None has the right to be worshipped but Allah"

  • Allah is the Creator of all that exists
  • Allah is the Proprietor of all that exists and the Disposer of all affairs
  • Allah is the One Who deserves to be worshipped alone

The meaning of the second part: "Muhammad is His slave and Messenger"

  • To obey the Messenger (peace be upon him) in what he ordered
  • To believe in everything he said
  • To refrain from what he prohibited and warned against
  • That you do not worship Allah except by what the Messenger (peace be upon him) legislated
2

The Prayer (Salah)

The prayer is a means through which a slave of Allah maintains a relationship between him/herself and his/her Lord. In it, the person privately converses with his/her Lord, seeking His forgiveness and asking Him for help and guidance.

Key Aspects of Prayer:

  • There are five prayers which must be performed daily
  • Men observe them in the Masjid (mosque) in congregation, except for those who have a valid excuse
  • Muslims come to know one another and build bonds of love and unity
  • All social differences such as class, race, and lineage are disregarded
  • Muslims line up side by side in straight rows, all facing one direction (Makkah)
  • All are rendered equal in their subservience to Allah
3

The Obligatory Charity (Zakat)

This charity is a small percentage of wealth which a rich Muslim gives to his less fortunate brothers and sisters. There are certain conditions under which it is to be given. A Muslim gives it willingly and fulfills the command of Allah when giving it out.

Goals of Zakat:

  • Revive mutual and social support among Muslims
  • Eradicate poverty and put an end to the dangers which result from it
  • Cleanse the hearts of the rich from greed
  • Cleanse the hearts of the poor from hate and jealousy
  • Create a caring society where the rich care for the needs of the poor

Linguistic meaning: 'Zakat' means 'an increase'. It increases one's wealth and safeguards it from misfortunes. Charities are rewarded graciously by Allah.

4

The Fast of Ramadan (Sawm)

It is obligatory upon every Muslim to fast once a year, for the entire month of Ramadan. From the break of dawn until the sun sets, Muslims must abstain from anything that breaks the fast, whether it is food, drink, or sexual intercourse.

"O you who have believed, fasting has been decreed for you as it was decreed upon those before you that you may become righteous."

— Quran 2:183

About Ramadan:

  • Fasting is an act of worship in Islam
  • It was an obligation legislated in previous religions as well
  • Muslims use the lunar calendar to determine the beginning and end of each month
  • The lunar calendar is used to determine religious seasons such as fasting and Hajj
5

The Pilgrimage (Hajj)

Hajj is the pilgrimage one makes to the Sacred House of Allah (the Ka'bah) in order to perform certain rites at specific places and times. This pillar of Islam is obligatory upon every Muslim, male or female, who is sane and has reached the age of puberty once in a lifetime, if they have the physical and financial capability to perform it.

"Pilgrimage to the House is a duty owed to Allah by all people who are able to undertake it. And as for those who deny the truth – verily, Allah does not stand in need of anything in all the worlds."

— Quran 3:97

The Significance of Hajj:

  • Hajj is the largest Islamic gathering
  • Muslims from all over the world come together in one place at one time
  • They all call upon the same Lord, wear the same clothes, and perform the same rituals
  • No difference is made between rich and poor, noble and ignoble, white and black, Arab and non-Arab
  • All are equal in front of Allah
  • There is no difference between them except in piety (taqwaa)
  • The Hajj emphasizes the brotherhood of all Muslims and the unity of their hopes and feelings
